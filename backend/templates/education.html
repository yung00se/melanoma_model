Recognizing Skin Lesions: Malignant vs Benign

Skin lesions, growths, and marks are common and often harmless. However, some can be warning signs of skin cancer. Understanding what to look for can help with early detection and potentially save lives.

Signs of Benign (Non-Cancerous) Lesions

  • Symmetrical shape
  • Even, smooth borders
  • One consistent color (brown, tan, or skin-colored)
  • Small size (typically less than 6 mm, about the size of a pencil eraser)
  • No significant change over time

Signs of Malignant (Potentially Cancerous) Lesions

  • Asymmetrical shape
  • Irregular, blurred, or jagged borders
  • Multiple colors (black, red, white, blue, uneven shades)
  • Diameter larger than 6 mm
  • Changes in size, color, or shape over time
  • Bleeding, itching, or crusting

The ABCDE Rule

The ABCDE rule is a helpful guide for checking moles or skin lesions for potential melanoma:

  • Asymmetry: One half is different from the other half.
  • Border: Edges are irregular, ragged, or blurred.
  • Color: Uneven colors or multiple shades.
  • Diameter: Larger than 6 mm (pencil eraser size).
  • Evolution: Changes in size, shape, color, or elevation.
